The Strokes

The Strokes are an American rock band from Manhattan, New York. Formed in 1998, the band is composed of singer Julian Casablancas, guitarists Nick Valensi and Albert Hammond Jr., bassist Nikolai Fraiture, and drummer Fabrizio Moretti. Following the conclusion of five-album deals with RCA and Rough Trade, the band has continued to release new music through Casablancas' Cult Records.

Albums

Is This It

Is This It is the debut studio album by American rock band the Strokes, first released on July 30, 2001, through RCA Records. It was recorded at Transporterraum in New York City with producer Gordon Raphael during March and April 2001. For their debut, the band strived to capture a simple sound that was not significantly enhanced in the studio. Building on the work of their 2001 debut EP, The Modern Age, the band members molded compositions largely through live takes during recording sessions, while songwriter Julian Casablancas continued to detail the lives and relationships of urban youth.

Room on Fire

Room on Fire is the second studio album by American rock band the Strokes, released on October 28, 2003, through RCA Records. The record follows their 2001 debut Is This It and includes three singles: "12:51", "Reptilia", and "The End Has No End". The album title Room on Fire was drawn from a line in "Reptilia": "The room is on fire as she's fixing her hair." Many critics at the time noted that the album was arguably an extension of the band's 2001 debut.

First Impressions of Earth

First Impressions of Earth is the third studio album by American rock band the Strokes. It was released on January 3, 2006, through RCA Records, having been preceded by lead single "Juicebox" some weeks earlier. It is also the first album by the Strokes to have a Parental Advisory label on the cover. The album was recorded over a ten-month period at Electric Lady Studios in New York City, with producer David Kahne. The album's cover photo is a Polaroid picture taken by bassist Nikolai Fraiture's girlfriend, Ilona Rubenstein.

Angles

Angles is the fourth studio album by American rock band the Strokes. It was released on March 18, 2011, through RCA Records. It is their first album since First Impressions of Earth (2006), their longest gap to date between studio albums. After touring in support of First Impressions of Earth, the Strokes went on an extended hiatus in 2007 and returned in 2009 with new material as well as a new touring line-up, with keyboardist and multi-instrumentalist Gustavo Dudamel and guitarist Nick Valensi's cousin, guitarist and keyboardist Jeff Kite, joining the band. The album's lead single, "Under Cover of Darkness", was released on February 9, 2011, and reached number 47 on the Billboard Hot 100.

Comedown Machine

Comedown Machine is the fifth studio album by American rock band the Strokes. It was released on March 26, 2013, through RCA Records. The band decided to pull a media blackout for the album, with no promotion in the form of television appearances, interviews, photoshoots, live shows, or tours. The album's cover is a painting called "The Fragile" by Peter Phillips from 1964. The album received generally favorable reviews from music critics, and peaked at number 10 on the Billboard 200.

The New Abnormal

The New Abnormal is the sixth studio album by American rock band the Strokes, released on April 10, 2020, through Cult and RCA Records. It was their first album in seven years, following Comedown Machine (2013), and their first release since the Future Present Past EP (2016). The album was recorded at Shangri-La Studios in Malibu, California, with producer Rick Rubin. The album's cover artwork is a painting by Jean-Michel Basquiat called Bird on Money.

The Modern Age

The Modern Age is the debut EP by American rock band the Strokes. It was released on January 29, 2001, through Rough Trade Records and was recorded during three days in December 2000. The EP comprises three songs, two of which were later featured on their debut studio album Is This It (2001). The EP was released to positive reviews, with critics complimenting the band's sound and frontman Julian Casablancas' vocals. The Modern Age was included in several publications' lists of the best albums of 2001 and the best albums of the 2000s.

Future Present Past

Future Present Past is an EP by American rock band the Strokes, released on June 3, 2016, through Cult Records. It contains three songs, "Drag Queen", "OBLIVIUS", and "Threat of Joy". The EP was recorded at the band's home studio in New York City, and produced by band member Gus Oberg. The EP received generally favorable reviews from music critics, and peaked at number 55 on the Billboard 200.

The Observer

The Observer is a compilation album by American rock band the Strokes, released in 2006 by RCA Records. The album comprises various B-sides from singles released from their albums Is This It (2001), Room on Fire (2003), and First Impressions of Earth (2006). The album was released without the band's involvement. The Observer received generally positive reviews from music critics, and peaked at number 70 on the UK Albums Chart.

Members

Julian Casablancas

Julian Fernando Casablancas is an American singer, musician, songwriter, and record producer. He is best known as the lead singer and primary songwriter of rock band the Strokes, which was formed in 1998. Casablancas has also released two solo albums: Phrazes for the Young (2009) and Tyranny (2014). In addition to his work in music, Casablancas has founded the Cult Records record label and a fashion line named NYCT clothing.

Nick Valensi

Nicholas Valensi is an American musician, singer, songwriter, and record producer. He is best known as the lead guitarist of the American rock band the Strokes, which was formed in 1998. Valensi has also performed in the side project CRX, and has produced and co-written songs for artists such as Regina Spektor and Sia.

Albert Hammond Jr.

Albert Louis Hammond Jr. is an American guitarist, singer, songwriter, and music producer. He is most famous for his role as rhythm and lead guitarist, as well as occasional keyboard player and backing vocalist, in the American rock band the Strokes. He is the son of singer-songwriter Albert Hammond.

Fabrizio Moretti

Fabrizio Moretti is an Italian-Brazilian musician and visual artist best known as the drummer for American indie rock band the Strokes. He is also a member of Brazilian/American rock supergroup Little Joy and garage rock band Machinegum.

Nikolai Fraiture

Nikolai Philippe Fraiture is an American musician, singer, songwriter, and record producer. He is best known as the bassist for the American rock band the Strokes, with whom he has recorded six studio albums. Fraiture has also performed in the bands Summer Moon and Nickel Eye, and has produced and co-written songs for artists such as Regina Spektor and Sia.
